Research Document for The Night Marketer Internship
Campaign Period: November 3, 2025 - February 3, 2026 (90 Days)

Executive Summary
This research document provides a comprehensive analysis of Google Ads fundamentals and performance metrics for ToolWorld India's 90-day advertising campaign. The campaign invested ₹1,37,720 across 9 active campaigns, generating 33,371 clicks and 1.19 million impressions. Our analysis reveals strong performance in CTR (4.23%, above industry average) and quality scores (78-94%), while identifying critical optimization opportunities in conversion tracking and budget allocation.
Understanding Google Ads Fundamentals
What is Google Ads?
Google Ads is Google's online advertising platform operating on a Pay-Per-Click (PPC) model. Businesses bid on keywords to display ads when users search for related terms. The auction system considers both bid amounts and ad quality to determine ad placement. Advertisers only pay when someone clicks their ad, making it a cost-effective way to reach potential customers actively searching for products or services.
Campaign Structure
Google Ads follows a four-level hierarchy that organizes advertising efforts:
•	Account Level: The top container holding all campaigns and billing information
•	Campaign Level: Defines budget, targeting, and bidding strategy (e.g., 'Power Supply all' with ₹24,532 budget)
•	Ad Group Level: Groups related keywords and ads by theme or product category
•	Keyword Level: Specific search terms you bid on that trigger your ads
Critical Performance Metrics
Click-Through Rate (CTR)
Formula: CTR = (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100
CTR measures the percentage of people who click your ad after seeing it. ToolWorld India achieved an average CTR of 4.23%, which is above the industry benchmark of 2-3% for search ads. This indicates strong ad relevance and appeal to the target audience. High CTR signals to Google that ads are valuable to users, resulting in better ad positions and lower costs over time.
Cost Per Click (CPC)
Formula: CPC = Total Cost ÷ Total Clicks
CPC represents the average amount paid each time someone clicks an ad. The campaign data shows CPC ranging from ₹2.07 to ₹7.50, with significant device variation (Mobile: ₹2.43 vs Desktop: ₹13.75). This metric directly impacts budget efficiency—lower CPC means more clicks for the same investment. CPC is influenced by keyword competition, ad quality score, and bidding strategy.
Quality Score
Quality Score (0-100%) evaluates expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page experience. ToolWorld's campaigns show healthy scores ranging from 78.1% to 94.3%, with an average around 85%. Higher quality scores lead to lower CPC and better ad positions. The 'Function Generator' campaign's excellent 94.3% score demonstrates optimal alignment between keywords, ads, and landing pages.

Timing and User Behavior Patterns
Time-of-Day Performance
Analysis reveals clear business-hours patterns. Peak performance occurs 9 AM - 11 AM with 70,878 to 116,143 impressions per hour. Performance remains strong throughout business hours (8 AM - 6 PM) but drops significantly after 8 PM. This pattern aligns with B2B purchasing behavior, as engineers and procurement managers actively search during work hours. Weekend traffic drops notably, with Sunday recording the lowest impressions (117,677) compared to Wednesday's peak (231,553).
Keywords vs Search Terms
Understanding the distinction between keywords and search terms is crucial. Keywords are what you bid on, while search terms are what users actually type. For example, bidding on the keyword 'multimeter' triggered various actual searches: 'uni t multimeter' (32 clicks, ₹56), 'unit multimeter' (21 clicks, ₹17), and 'multimeter' (5 clicks, ₹13). This demonstrates how broad match keywords can capture relevant variations but also highlights the need for regular search term review to identify and exclude irrelevant traffic through negative keywords.
Key Findings and Recommendations
Strengths
•	Above-average CTR of 4.23% indicates strong ad relevance and copy quality
•	Healthy quality scores (78-94%) demonstrate good alignment between keywords, ads, and landing pages
•	Efficient mobile performance with 84% of clicks at ₹2.43 CPC
•	Well-structured campaigns targeting relevant product categories
Critical Issues
•	Zero conversions recorded: This is the most critical issue requiring immediate attention. Without conversion tracking, ROI cannot be measured accurately
•	High cross-network spend: 93% of budget goes to cross-network campaigns, which may have lower conversion rates than Google Search
•	Desktop CPC disparity: At ₹13.75 per click versus ₹2.43 on mobile, desktop efficiency needs investigation
Immediate Actions (Week 1)
•	Implement or verify conversion tracking to enable ROI measurement
•	Review search term reports and add 20-30 negative keywords to exclude irrelevant traffic
•	Pause campaigns with CTR below 1.5% until optimization can be implemented
Short-term Optimizations (Month 1)
•	Increase mobile bid adjustments by +30% to capitalize on efficient mobile performance
•	Set up time-of-day bid scheduling: increase bids 9 AM - 5 PM weekdays, reduce after 8 PM
•	Reallocate 20-30% of budget from underperforming campaigns to top performers like 'Fume extractors' and 'Oscilloscope - Targeted States'
Conclusion
ToolWorld India's Google Ads campaign demonstrates solid fundamentals with above-average CTR, healthy quality scores, and efficient mobile performance. The campaign structure is sound, targeting relevant industrial equipment categories with appropriate budgets. However, the absence of conversion tracking represents a critical gap that prevents accurate ROI assessment and limits optimization opportunities.
The path forward is clear: implement conversion tracking immediately, optimize budget allocation toward proven performers, and leverage timing patterns through strategic bid adjustments. With these improvements, the campaign is well-positioned to deliver measurable business results while maintaining its current efficiency advantages.
